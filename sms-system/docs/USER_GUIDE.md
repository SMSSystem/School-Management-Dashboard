## Part 1 — Getting Started (All Users)

### 1.1 Logging In

1. Open the application in your web browser.
2. On the login screen, enter your **Email** and **Password**.
3. Click **Sign In**.
4. You will be redirected to your home dashboard automatically. The dashboard content you see depends on your role.

If you cannot log in, contact your Institution Administrator to confirm your account details or reset your password.

---

### 1.2 The Dashboard Layout

Once logged in, the screen is divided into two areas:

- **Sidebar (left):** The main navigation menu. Click any item to go to that section. Items shown depend on your role — you will only see sections you have access to. On smaller screens the sidebar collapses to icon-only view.
- **Main area (right):** The current page content.
- **Top bar (Navbar):** Displays your name on the right side. Also contains the theme toggle and the sign-out button.

Navigation is grouped into sections (e.g., **PEOPLE**, **CURRICULUM**, **TIMETABLE**, **OUTCOMES**, **ATTENDANCE**, **OTHER**).

---

### 1.3 Your User Profile

Your profile page lets you view and update your personal contact information.

1. In the sidebar, click **User Profile** (under the **OTHER** section).
2. Your name, email, phone number, and role are displayed.
3. To update your **Name** or **Phone**, click the **Edit** button, make your changes, and click **Save**.
4. Your **Email** cannot be changed here — contact your administrator if your email needs updating.

---

### 1.4 Light and Dark Mode

The application supports both light mode and dark mode.

1. Click the **sun / moon icon** in the top-right corner of the Navbar to toggle between modes.
2. Your preference is saved automatically and remembered the next time you open the application.

---

### 1.5 Logging Out

1. Click the **sign-out icon** in the top-right corner of the Navbar.
2. You will be returned to the login screen immediately.

---

### 1.6 Inactivity Auto-Logout

If you are inactive for **5 minutes**, the application will sign you out automatically. Any unsaved changes will be lost. You will need to sign in again to continue.

---

## Part 2 — Institution Admin

As an Institution Admin, you have full access to all data within your school. This section covers every feature available to your role.

---

### 2.1 Setting Up Your Institution Profile

The Institution Profile stores your school's details and is required before you can generate report cards. A small amber dot appears next to the **Institution Profile** sidebar link until setup is complete.

1. In the sidebar, click **Institution Profile** (under **OTHER**).
2. The setup wizard has **7 steps**. Complete each step and click **Next** to proceed. You can also click **Back** to return to a previous step.

| Step | What to fill in |
|------|----------------|
| 1 — Basic Info | Institution name (required) and optional motto |
| 2 — Contact | Phone number, email address, and physical address |
| 3 — Logo | Upload your institution logo (PNG or JPEG). The image is resized automatically. |
| 4 — Signature | Upload an image of the authorised signatory's signature, or type a name as a text signature |
| 5 — Role Labels | Customise the staff role titles that appear on report cards (e.g., change "Principal" to "Headmaster") |
| 6 — Grading System | Choose **Flat** (all subjects equally weighted) or **Weighted** (subjects carry different weights) |
| 7 — Review | Confirm all entered details before saving |

3. On the **Review** step, click **Save** to finalise the profile.
4. The amber dot disappears once the profile is marked complete.

You can return to this page at any time to update your details. Changes take effect on the next report card generated.

---

### 2.2 Creating User Accounts

New accounts for teachers, students, and parents must be created by an Institution Admin.

1. In the sidebar, click **Create User** (under **PEOPLE**).
2. Select the **Role** for the new user: **Teacher** (Regular or Senior), **Student**, or **Parent**.
3. Fill in the required fields:
   - All roles: **Full Name**, **Email**, **Password**
   - Teachers: additional fields for staff information
   - Students: additional fields for student information
   - Parents: link to their child's student account
4. Click **Create** to create the account. The user can now sign in using the email and password you set.

> **Note:** Email addresses must be unique across the platform. Passwords should be communicated securely to the new user, who should change their password on first sign-in.

---

### 2.3 Managing Teachers

The Teachers list shows all teacher accounts registered to your institution.

1. In the sidebar, click **Teachers** (under **PEOPLE**).
2. Use the **search bar** at the top to filter by name or other fields.
3. The list is paginated — click the page numbers at the bottom to navigate.

**To create a teacher**, use **Create User** (see Section 2.2) rather than the Teachers list directly.

**To edit a teacher's details:**
1. Click the **pencil (edit) icon** on the teacher's row.
2. Update the fields in the modal and click **Update**.

**To delete a teacher:**
1. Click the **trash (delete) icon** on the teacher's row.
2. Confirm the deletion in the modal.

> Deleting a teacher account is permanent. Ensure the teacher has no outstanding records before deleting.

---

### 2.4 Managing Students

The Students list works identically to the Teachers list.

1. In the sidebar, click **Students** (under **PEOPLE**).
2. Search, edit, or delete student records using the same controls described in Section 2.3.

To create a new student account, use **Create User** (see Section 2.2).

---

### 2.5 Managing Parents

1. In the sidebar, click **Parents** (under **PEOPLE**).
2. Parent records are created via **Create User** (see Section 2.2), where you link the parent to their child's student account.
3. Edit and delete controls follow the same pattern as Sections 2.3 and 2.4.

---

### 2.6 Managing Subjects

Subjects are the academic disciplines taught at your institution (e.g., Mathematics, English, Biology). Each subject has a **Coursework weight** and an **Exam weight**, which determine how final grades are calculated.

1. In the sidebar, click **Subjects** (under **CURRICULUM**).
2. Click the **+** button in the top-right corner to add a new subject.
3. Fill in the **Subject Name**, **Coursework weight (%)**, and **Exam weight (%)**.
   - The two weights must sum to 100.
4. Click **Create**.

To edit or delete a subject, use the pencil or trash icon on the subject's row.

> Subjects must be set up before you can record results or generate report cards for them.

---

### 2.7 Managing Departments

Departments group subjects and teachers under a head of department (Senior Teacher).

1. In the sidebar, click **Departments** (under **CURRICULUM**).
2. Click **+** to create a new department. Enter a **Department Name** and optionally assign a **Head Teacher**.
3. Click **Create**.

Edit and delete controls follow the standard pattern.

---

### 2.8 Managing Houses

Houses are pastoral groups that students can be assigned to. House membership appears on report cards.

1. In the sidebar, click **Houses** (under **CURRICULUM**).
2. Click **+** to create a new house. Enter a **House Name** and optional additional details.
3. Click **Create**.
4. Click on a house row (or its detail link) to view the students assigned to that house.

---

### 2.9 Managing Classes

Classes are the teaching groups that students are enrolled in and teachers are assigned to.

1. In the sidebar, click **Classes** (under **CURRICULUM**).
2. Click **+** to create a new class. Enter the **Class Name** and assign a **Teacher** and any **Students**.
3. Click **Create**.

Edit and delete controls follow the standard pattern.

---

### 2.10 Managing Terms

Terms define the academic periods used for attendance, results, and report cards.

1. In the sidebar, click **Terms** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new term. Enter the **Term Name**, **Start Date**, and **End Date**.
3. Click **Create**.

> Terms are also set up automatically if you use the Academic Calendar wizard (see Section 2.11). Do not create duplicate terms for the same period.

---

### 2.11 Academic Calendar

The Academic Calendar sets up your school's academic year structure, including the start and end dates of each term and any non-school days (public holidays and closures). Attendance calculations depend on this calendar.

1. In the sidebar, click **Academic Calendar** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. If no academic year has been created yet, you will see a wizard to create one:
   - Enter the **Academic Year Start Date** and **End Date**.
   - The system suggests term start and end dates automatically, adjusting for weekends and any Jamaican public holidays detected. You can override any date manually.
   - Name each term (e.g., "Term 1", "First Term") or accept the suggested names.
   - Click **Create Academic Year** to save.
3. Once an academic year exists, the calendar view shows:
   - The three terms for the selected year.
   - All non-school days (public holidays and manually added closures) highlighted.
4. To add a custom non-school day (e.g., a school closure or internal event), click the relevant date on the calendar and provide a description.

> Accurate academic calendar data is essential for the General Attendance Register and Attendance Summary Register. Set it up before recording attendance.

---

### 2.12 Schedule

The Schedule page shows a weekly timetable view.

1. In the sidebar, click **Schedule** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Navigate between weeks using the arrows at the top.
3. Lesson blocks are displayed on their scheduled day and time.

---

### 2.13 Lessons

Lessons record what is being taught in each class session.

1. In the sidebar, click **Lessons** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new lesson. Fill in the required fields (subject, class, teacher, day, time).
3. Click **Create**.

Edit and delete controls follow the standard pattern.

---

### 2.14 Exams

Exams are scheduled assessments visible to students, parents, and all teacher roles.

1. In the sidebar, click **Exams** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new exam. Enter the subject, class, date, and any additional details.
3. Click **Create**.

> Only admins can delete exams. Teachers can create and edit exams for their assigned classes.

---

### 2.15 Assignments

Assignments are coursework tasks due by a specific date, visible to students and parents.

1. In the sidebar, click **Assignments** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new assignment. Enter the subject, class, due date, and description.
3. Click **Create**.

> Only admins can delete assignments. Teachers can create and edit their own.

---

### 2.16 Results

Results record a student's score on a specific assessment (coursework or exam).

1. In the sidebar, click **Results** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. Click **+** to add a new result.
3. Fill in:
   - **Student** — select the student.
   - **Subject** — select the subject.
   - **Term** — select the term this result belongs to.
   - **Assessment Type** — **Coursework** or **Exam**.
   - **Assessment Name** — a descriptive label (e.g., "Mid-term Test 1").
   - **Score** and **Max Score**.
4. Click **Create**.

To update a score, click the pencil icon on the result's row.

> Results feed directly into report card generation. Coursework and exam results are averaged separately and then combined according to the subject's weighting.

---

### 2.17 Feedback Comments

Feedback comments allow teachers to record a conduct grade and a comment for each student in each subject, per term. These comments appear on the report card.

1. In the sidebar, click **Feedback** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. You can view all feedback comments submitted by teachers across your institution.
3. To add or update a feedback comment:
   - Click **+** or the edit icon.
   - Select the **Student**, **Subject**, and **Term**.
   - Choose a **Conduct Grade** (A through E, or S/U).
   - Select a **Preset Comment** from the dropdown, or type a free-text comment in the **Comment** field. At least one of these is required. If both are provided, the free-text comment takes precedence.
4. Click **Save**.

---

### 2.18 Report Card Comments

Report Card Comments are section-level written comments added by supervisory staff for each student, separate from subject-level feedback.

1. In the sidebar, click **Report Card Comments** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. Select a **Class** and a **Term** from the dropdowns at the top.
3. A list of students in that class appears.
4. Click **Edit** on a student's row to open the comment editor.
5. The comment fields correspond to the role labels set in your institution profile (e.g., **Class Supervisor**, **Grade Supervisor**, **Principal**, **Vice Principal**).
6. Fill in as many comments as applicable and click **Save**.

These comments are pulled into the report card when it is generated.

---

### 2.19 Report Cards

The Report Cards page is where you generate, view, and download student report cards as PDFs.

#### Viewing existing report cards

1. In the sidebar, click **Report Cards** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. The table lists all generated report cards for your institution, showing student name, term, class, GPA, and date generated.
3. Click the **PDF icon** on a row to open the PDF preview modal. From there you can download the PDF.

#### Generating a single report card

1. On the Report Cards page, click **Generate Report Card**.
2. Select **Single Student**.
3. Choose a **Student** and a **Term** from the dropdowns.
4. Click **Generate**.
5. If a report card already exists for that student and term, it will be regenerated (updated) rather than duplicated.
6. Any warnings (e.g., missing results for a subject, missing attendance data) are shown after generation. Review these and correct the underlying data if needed.

#### Generating report cards in batch (whole class)

1. On the Report Cards page, click **Generate Report Card**.
2. Select **Batch (Class)**.
3. Choose a **Class** and a **Term**.
4. Click **Generate All**.
5. A progress bar tracks generation. Each student in the class is processed in turn.
6. After all cards are generated, class ranks and class averages are calculated and written automatically.
7. Any errors or warnings per student are shown in the results summary.

#### The PDF layout

Each report card PDF is a single A4 landscape page designed to fold into a four-panel pamphlet:
- **Front Cover:** Institution logo, student name, class, term, and house.
- **Inner Left:** Attendance summary, extracurricular activities, positions of responsibility, and section comments from supervisory staff.
- **Inner Right:** Subject results table (coursework %, exam %, final %, letter grade, conduct, teacher, and comment number), student average, class average, class rank, and GPA.
- **Back Cover:** Institution details, motto, and authorised signature.

> **Grade calculation note:** If only one component (coursework or exam) has results for a subject, the final grade is calculated entirely from the available component — the missing component is not treated as zero.

---

### 2.20 General Attendance Register

The General Attendance Register is the primary tool for recording daily student attendance by class, in morning (AM) and afternoon (PM) sessions.

1. In the sidebar, click **General Register** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. If prompted by the scope modal, select the **Class** and **Term** you want to record attendance for.
3. The register displays the current week by default, with one column per school day (non-school days are skipped automatically based on your Academic Calendar).
4. Use the week navigation arrows to move to a different week.
5. For each student row, click the attendance button for a given day and session to cycle through states:

   | Code | Meaning |
   |------|---------|
   | P | Present |
   | A | Absent |
   | L | Late |
   | S | Sick |
   | E | Excused |

6. For an **Excused** absence, a popover appears where you can record the reason.
7. Attendance is saved as a **draft** automatically as you click. Draft data is stored in your browser temporarily.
8. Click **Submit** to commit the draft to the database. The submit button is only available during the current active session window.

> Sessions from past dates can be edited but within a limited time window. Sessions too far in the past are locked.

---

### 2.21 Attendance Summary Register

The Attendance Summary Register provides a read-only overview of all students' attendance across an entire month, suitable for exporting as a printable PDF.

1. In the sidebar, click **Summary Register** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select the **Class**, **Term**, and **Month** you want to view.
3. The gridsheet displays each student as a row and each school day of the selected month as a column, with AM and PM attendance codes.
4. To export the gridsheet as a PDF, click **Export PDF**. The PDF is formatted for A3 landscape printing.

> Attendance data in the summary register is drawn from pre-computed summaries. If the data appears stale or incorrect, use the **Rebuild Summaries** utility (see Section 2.22).

---

### 2.22 Administrative Utilities

These tools are for maintenance tasks and are not part of daily workflow.

#### Rebuild Attendance Summaries

If attendance summary data is out of sync (e.g., after bulk edits), use this tool to recompute it.

1. In the sidebar, click **Rebuild Summaries** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select the **Class** and **Term** to rebuild.
3. Click **Rebuild**. The system recomputes all summary records for that class and term from the raw attendance documents.

> Run this tool if the Attendance Summary Register or report card attendance figures appear incorrect.

#### Backfill Student Classes

This utility links existing student accounts to their correct classes based on stored class assignments. Use it if students were created before their class records existed.

1. In the sidebar, click **Backfill Classes** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Follow the on-screen instructions to run the backfill.

## Part 3 — Teachers

Teachers in this system have two sub-roles with slightly different access:

- **Regular Teacher** — access limited to classes and students they are assigned to teach.
- **Senior Teacher** — all regular teacher access plus departmental oversight of all classes within their department, and access to the General Attendance Register and Summary Register.

The instructions in this section apply to both roles unless otherwise noted.

---

### 3.1 Your Dashboard

Your home dashboard shows:
- A **weekly schedule** (BigCalendar view) of your assigned classes and lessons.
- **Announcements** from your institution.

Senior Teachers additionally see departmental overview widgets (check back as these continue to be expanded).

---

### 3.2 People Directory

Teachers can view lists of teachers, students, and parents at their institution.

1. In the sidebar, click **Teachers**, **Students**, or **Parents** (under **PEOPLE**).
2. Use the search bar to find specific individuals.
3. Click on a name to view their detail page.

> Teachers have read-only access to these lists. Only admins can create, edit, or delete accounts.

---

### 3.3 Schedule

1. In the sidebar, click **Schedule** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. The weekly calendar displays your assigned lessons.
3. Navigate between weeks using the arrows at the top.

---

### 3.4 Lessons

You can create and edit lessons for the classes you teach.

1. In the sidebar, click **Lessons** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new lesson for one of your assigned classes.
3. Fill in the details and click **Create**.
4. Click the pencil icon to edit an existing lesson.

> Only admins can delete lessons.

---

### 3.5 Exams and Assignments

You can create and manage exams and assignments for your classes. Students and parents can view these.

**Exams:**
1. Click **Exams** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to schedule a new exam. Fill in the subject, class, and date.
3. Click **Create**.

**Assignments:**
1. Click **Assignments** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Click **+** to add a new assignment. Fill in the subject, class, due date, and description.
3. Click **Create**.

> Only admins can delete exams or assignments.

---

### 3.6 Recording Results

You can enter student scores for your assigned classes.

1. In the sidebar, click **Results** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. Click **+** to add a result.
3. Select the **Student**, **Subject**, **Term**, and **Assessment Type** (Coursework or Exam).
4. Enter the **Assessment Name**, **Score**, and **Max Score**.
5. Click **Create**.

To correct an error, click the pencil icon on the result's row.

> Scores are recorded as a percentage of the max score. For example, 36/40 is recorded as Score: 36, Max Score: 40 — not as 90.

---

### 3.7 Submitting Feedback Comments

Feedback comments must be submitted for each student you teach, for each term, before report cards are generated.

1. In the sidebar, click **Feedback** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. Click **+** to add a new comment, or click the pencil icon to edit an existing one.
3. Select the **Student**, **Subject**, and **Term**.
4. Choose a **Conduct Grade** from the dropdown:

   | Grade | Meaning |
   |-------|---------|
   | A | Outstanding |
   | B | Commendable |
   | C | Satisfactory |
   | D | Disruption |
   | E | Unsatisfactory |

5. Select a **Preset Comment** from the dropdown (20 options available), or write a free-text comment in the **Comment** field. At least one is required. If you fill in both, the free-text comment will appear on the report card.
6. Click **Save**.

> Submit feedback before the term closes. Once a report card is generated, it captures the comments that exist at that point in time.

---

### 3.8 Report Cards

All teacher roles can view and download generated report cards.

1. In the sidebar, click **Report Cards** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. The table shows all report cards for students accessible to your role.
3. Click the **PDF icon** on a row to open the PDF preview and download the report card.

> Only Institution Admins can generate report cards.

---

### 3.9 Attendance Register (Senior Teachers)

Senior Teachers can access the General Attendance Register and Attendance Summary Register.

**General Register:**
1. Click **General Register** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select the class and term to work with.
3. Record attendance using the P / A / L / S / E buttons for each student, day, and session (AM/PM).
4. Click **Submit** to save the session.

**Summary Register:**
1. Click **Summary Register** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select class, term, and month.
3. View the gridsheet or click **Export PDF** for a printable summary.

For full instructions on these features, refer to Sections 2.20 and 2.21.

---

### 3.10 Subject Attendance Register (Regular Teachers)

Regular Teachers have access to the Subject Attendance Register, which tracks attendance per subject session rather than per school day.

1. In the sidebar, click **Subject Register** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select the **Subject** and **Class** you are taking.
3. The register shows each student in the class.
4. Mark each student as **Present**, **Absent**, **Late**, or **Excused** for this session.
5. Click **Submit** to save.

---

- **Announcements** from the institution.

If you have more than one child linked to your account, contact your Institution Administrator to confirm both are linked correctly.

---

### 5.2 Schedule

1. In the sidebar, click **Schedule** (under **TIMETABLE**).
2. Your child's weekly timetable is displayed.
3. Navigate between weeks using the arrows at the top.

### 5.3 Exams and Assignments

You can view upcoming exams and assignments for your child's classes.

1. Click **Exams** (under **TIMETABLE**) to see scheduled exams.
2. Click **Assignments** (under **TIMETABLE**) to see coursework tasks and their due dates.

These lists are read-only.

---

### 5.4 Results

You can view the academic results recorded for your child.

1. In the sidebar, click **Results** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. Results are listed by subject, assessment, and term.
3. Use the search bar to filter.

Results are read-only. Contact the school if you have a query about a specific score.

---

### 5.5 Report Cards

You can view and download your child's generated report cards.

1. In the sidebar, click **Report Cards** (under **OUTCOMES**).
2. The list shows all report cards generated for your child.
3. Click the **PDF icon** on a row to open the report card.
4. Click **Download** to save the PDF.

---

### 5.6 Your Child's Attendance

1. In the sidebar, click **Child Attendance** (under **ATTENDANCE**).
2. Select the **Term** and **Month** to view your child's attendance for that period.
3. The register shows each school day with AM and PM session attendance codes.

Attendance records are read-only. Contact the school if you have a question about a specific record.

---

### 5.7 Institution Information

1. In the sidebar, click **Institution Info** (under **OTHER**).
2. The page displays the school's contact details and profile information in read-only mode.
